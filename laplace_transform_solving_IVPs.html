Solving Initial Value Problems | Topic Playlist

Initial value problems (IVPs) can be solved using the Laplace Transform. The Laplace Transform of derivative of the unknown function $y^{(n)}(t)$ is turned into $Y(s)$. Solving for $Y(s)$ are applying the inverse Laplace Transform will give the solution to the initial IVP.

Laplace Transform $\mathcal{L}\{ y^{(n)}(t) \}$ | Explanation Video

The Laplace Transform of $y(t)$, $y'(t)$, and $y''(t)$ are

\begin{equation} \mathcal{L}\{ y(t) \} = Y(s) \end{equation} \begin{equation} \mathcal{L}\{ y'(t) \} = sY(s) - y(0) \end{equation} \begin{equation} \mathcal{L}\{ y''(t) \} = s^{2}Y(s) - sy(0) - y'(0) \end{equation}

where $y(0)$ and $y'(0)$ are from the initial conditions of the initial value problem.

In general, the Laplace Transform of $y^{(n)}(t)$ is

\begin{equation} \mathcal{L}\{ y^{(n)}(t) \} = s^{n}Y(s) - s^{n-1}y(0) - s^{n-2}y'(0) - \cdots - sy^{(n-2)}(0) - y^{(n-1)}(0) \end{equation}

where $y(0),y'(0),\ldots,y^{(n-1)}(0)$ are from the initial conditions of the initial value problem.
