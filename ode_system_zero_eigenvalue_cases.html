Zero Eigenvalue Cases | Topic Playlist

The cases with at least one zero eigenvalue is one of several solution types for a two dimensional system of linear, constant coefficient, homogeneous differential equations. The types of solutions are called stable non-isolated fixed points, unstable non-isolated fixed points, and constant solution.

Solution | Explanation Video

When the system,

\begin{equation} \vec{x}' = A\vec{x} \qquad 0 = \lambda_{1} \neq \lambda_{2} \end{equation}

has a zero eigenvalue and a nonzero eigenvalue, the solution is the same as for distinct real nonzero eigenvalues.

\begin{equation} \vec{x}(t) = c_{1}\vec{v}_{1} + c_{2}e^{\lambda_{2}t}\vec{v}_{2} \end{equation}

Where the eigenvectors are $\vec{v}_{1}$ and $\vec{v}_{2}$.

When both eigenvalues are zero, the solution is the same as a star with only one possible 2x2 coefficient matrix with every vector being an eigenvector.

\begin{equation*} \begin{bmatrix} x' \\ y' \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 \end{bmatrix}\begin{bmatrix} x \\ y \end{bmatrix} \end{equation*} \begin{equation} \begin{bmatrix} x(t) \\ y(t) \end{bmatrix} = c_{1}\begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix} + c_{2}\begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} c_{1} \\ c_{2} \end{bmatrix} \end{equation}

Phase Portraits | Explanation Video

For the $0 = \lambda_{1} \neq \lambda_{2}$ case, the solution is called non-isolated fixed points and the stability is determined by the sign of the nonzero eigenvalue. Unlike a typical solution where the only stationary solution is the origin, all points along the eigendirection for the zero eigenvalue are stationary, so the fixed points are non-isolated.

For positive nonzero eigenvalue, the solution type is unstable non-isolated fixed points. Notice one of the eigendirections does not have any arrows since initial conditions on that eigendirection are stationary.

For negative nonzero eigenvalue, the solution type is stable non-isolated fixed points. Notice one of the eigendirections does not have any arrows since initial conditions on that eigendirection are stationary.

For the $0 = \lambda_{1} = \lambda_{2}$, the solution type is a constant solution since the derivatives are always zero. Solutions do not change from the initial condition.

Example 1 | Solution Video

Solve the following IVP, then draw a phase portrait.

\begin{equation*} \begin{bmatrix} x' \\ y' \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 \end{bmatrix}\begin{bmatrix} x \\ y \end{bmatrix} \quad \begin{bmatrix} x(0) \\ y(0) \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 3 \\ -1 \end{bmatrix} \end{equation*}
